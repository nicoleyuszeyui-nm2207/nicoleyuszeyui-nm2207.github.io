Background information on the Olympics!


1. The Summer Olympics (what we will be focusing on in this data story) is a leading international sports event that brings together athletes from all over the world in one host country to compete in their respective sports.

2. The first Summer Olympics was held in Athens,Greece in the year1896.

3. The Summer Olympics is held every 4 years.

4. The Summer Olympics have been held in 19 countries across 5 continents thus far.

5. The Olympic symbol consisting of 5 interlaced rings (blue, yellow, black, green, and white represents the union of the 5 continents at the game.

Berlin 1936: The Nazi Olympics

In 1931, Berlin was voted to host the 1936 Summer Olympics. However, the Nazi Party started rising to power in Germany in 1933 when Adolf Hitler became the sole dictator who controlled the country. Hitler idealized racial purity and racial struggle, thus these ideas drove government policies when he became the dictator of Germany. The ideology of anti-semitism in which the Jews were pictured as an inferior race, and the concept of the "Aryan" race being the most superior and master race, were widespread across the nation. Many Western countries disapproved of the racist ideologies that existed within the Nazis and proposed to boycott the Berlin Olympics in 1936. Despite this, it was surprising that 49 countries participated in the Summer Olympics that year, which was the most number of countries that participated compared to previous years.



What went down at the Nazi Olympics?

In the Nazi Olympics, Jesse Owens and Ralph Metcalfe were the two most decorated athletes in the Athletics sport. Jesse Owens brought home 4 gold medals and Ralph Metcalfe secured 1 Gold and 1 Silver medal. Both of them were African-Americans and Jesse Owens in particular, was known to be the star of the games who destroyed Germany‚Äôs attempt at proving Aryan superiority.

Boycotts??

In the years 1956,1964,1976,1980,1984 and 1988, many countries boycotted/ did not attend the Olympic Games. Many boycotts happened due to political reasons. In particular, the 1976 Montreal Olympic Games observed 34 countries' non-attendance and the 1980 Moscow Olympic Games observed 66 countries' non-attendance.

In recent years, fewer boycotts were observed in the Summer Olympics as the International Olympic Committee (IOC) stressed the importance of separating the games and politics. It is important to recognise that the purpose of the Olympic Games is to observe sporting excellence and unify athletes regardless of the country they represent. Despite the emphasis the IOC places on this, the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics was still boycotted by several nations including USA and Canada. The nations did not send any minsters and officials to attend the games that year to protest against the Chinese mistreatment of the Uyghur population.

Something very interesting...

Let us take a look at this line graph. It depicts the percentage of medals won by each host country from 1992-2016. To give an example, the orange line shows the percentage of medals won by Australia in every Olympic game from 1992-2016. The big red point on this line indicates that Australia hosted the Olympics in the year 2000. If we look at it closely, Australia managed to secure a greater percentage of medals in the year 2000 compared to other years. The same goes for Greece in the year 2004 and China in the year 2008 when they hosted the Olympics. For the other host countries, they managed to secure a relatively higher percentage of medals in the year that they hosted the games compared to other years.

A specific term for this trend is called the Home field advanage, where Olympic athletes from the host country bags more medals than when they compete away from home. Some reasons for this trend are:

1) Competing at home means a larger and more supportive crowd, thus relaying positive energies to home team athletes.

2) Not needing to travel on long flights to other countries to compete help home team athletes feel more accustomed and comfortable to competition facilities and practices.

3) Host countries may send bigger groups of athletes to compete as home team athletes only need to meet lower qualification standards, thus increasing their possibilities of winning medals.
